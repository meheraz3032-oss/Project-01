Our Departments

Computer Technology

Computer technology is the use of computers and related devices to process, store, and communicate information, encompassing both hardware and software. It includes the design, development, and application of computer systems, networks, and software to solve problems, automate tasks, and connect people globally.

Civil Technology

Civil technology is the application of engineering principles and practices to the design, construction, and maintenance of the built environment, including infrastructure such as roads, bridges, buildings, and water supply systems. It encompasses various disciplines, including structural, geotechnical, environmental, and transportation engineering.

Mechanical Technology

Mechanical technology is the application of engineering principles and practices to the design, analysis, and manufacturing of mechanical systems. It encompasses various disciplines, including thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, materials science, and structural analysis.

Electrical Technology

Electrical technology is the application of engineering principles and practices to the design, development, and maintenance of electrical systems and equipment. It encompasses various disciplines, including power generation, transmission, distribution, and utilization, as well as control systems and electronics.

Electronics Technology

Electronics technology is the application of engineering principles and practices to the design, development, and maintenance of electronic systems and devices. It encompasses various disciplines, including circuit design, signal processing, and telecommunications.

Power Technology

Power technology is an applied engineering field that deals with the generation, transmission, distribution, and utilization of electric power, as well as the design and application of associated equipment and systems. It draws heavily from both electrical and mechanical engineering principles and is crucial for powering modern society.
